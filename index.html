Getting There

Logistics & Transit

Travel from Gujarat to Kerala involves a long but beautiful coastal train ride, or a fast flight connection into Kochi (Cochin). Choose the best option for your group.

✈️
Flight — Recommended
Route: Surat (STV) → Kochi (COK)
Duration: 2.5–4 hrs (connecting via BOM/BLR)
Why: Saves two full days of travel. Arrive fresh in Kochi to start the expedition immediately.
💰 ₹5,000–₹9,000 one-way
🚂
Train — Scenic Coast
Route: Surat → Ernakulam Jn. (16382 or 16346)
Duration: ~36–42 hrs along the Konkan Coast
Why: A budget-friendly option with beautiful views of Goan rivers and Karnataka forests.
💰 ₹900–₹3,200 (SL to 2A)

The Grand Circuit

10-Day Itinerary

An extended, unhurried journey through Kerala's absolute best offerings. From colonial streets to jungle safaris, backwater houseboats, and golden shores.

01
Arrival & Heritage
Surat → Fort Kochi
  • Transit: Arrive at Kochi (COK) via flight. Transfer to a boutique heritage hotel in Fort Kochi.
  • Afternoon: Walk the cobblestone streets. Visit the 16th-century Pardesi Synagogue and the intricate murals of the Dutch Palace in Jew Town.
  • Sunset: Watch the iconic, giant cantilevered Chinese Fishing Nets being lowered into the Arabian Sea at Vasco da Gama Square.
  • Dinner: Fresh Arabian Sea catch cooked right on the promenade, paired with Kerala parotta.
02
The Ascent (130 km)
Kochi → Munnar
  • Morning: Begin the 4-hour scenic drive up the winding Western Ghats. The temperature drops noticeably as you climb.
  • En Route: Stop at the spectacular Cheeyappara and Valara Waterfalls roaring down the mountainside.
  • Afternoon: Check into a hillside resort. Visit the KDHP Tata Tea Museum to learn the history of Nilgiri tea and taste fresh brews.
  • Evening: Relax in the cool mountain air (often dropping to 12-15°C).
03
High Altitude Exploration
Munnar Peaks & Wildlife
  • Early Morning: Take a government bus safari into Eravikulam National Park to spot the endangered Nilgiri Tahr mountain goats roaming the slopes.
  • Mid-day: Visit Mattupetty Dam for speed boating on the pristine lake, and shout your name at Echo Point for perfect valley acoustics.
  • Afternoon: Drive up to Top Station (the highest point in Munnar) for panoramic views stretching into the neighboring state of Tamil Nadu.
04
The Spice Capital (90 km)
Munnar → Thekkady
  • Morning: Drive down to Thekkady. The air here smells intensely of cardamom, cloves, and black pepper.
  • Afternoon: Take a guided Spice Plantation tour. See vanilla vines, cinnamon bark, and nutmeg growing in their natural wild habitat.
  • Evening: Attend a thrilling Kalaripayattu (ancient martial arts) show or a vibrant Kathakali dance-drama performance in Kumily town.
05
Periyar Tiger Reserve
Jungle Safari & Ayurveda
  • Early Morning: Take the famous boat safari on Periyar Lake. This is your best chance to spot wild elephant herds, bison, and sambar deer drinking at the water's edge.
  • Alternative: For adventure seekers, opt for a guided jungle trek or bamboo rafting deep into the tiger reserve.
  • Afternoon: Give your body a rest. Indulge in an authentic 90-minute Kerala Ayurvedic Abhyanga massage using medicated warm oils.
06
Venice of the East (140 km)
Thekkady → Alleppey Houseboat
  • 12:00 PM: Drive down from the hills to Alleppey. Board your private Kettuvallam (traditional wooden houseboat).
  • Cruise: Glide slowly through the vast Vembanad Lake, narrow palm-fringed canals, and paddy fields located below sea level.
  • Dining: Enjoy traditional meals (Karimeen fry, tapioca, banana fritters) cooked fresh by your personal onboard chef.
  • Night: The boat docks at sunset. Sleep on the gentle waters under the stars—an unforgettable experience.
07
Giant Bird & Cliff Beach (110 km)
Alleppey → Jatayu → Varkala
  • Morning: Disembark houseboat after breakfast. Drive south along the coastal highway.
  • En Route: Stop at Jatayu Earth's Center. Take the modern cable car to the mountaintop to see the world's largest bird sculpture.
  • Afternoon: Arrive at Varkala — India's only cliff-side beach.
  • Evening: Watch the sunset from the bohemian North Cliff cafes with stunning Arabian sea panoramas below.
08
Mangroves & Golden Sands (60 km)
Varkala → Poovar Island
  • Morning: Relax on Varkala beach or take a surfing lesson in the gentle waves.
  • Afternoon: Drive further south to Poovar Island, a unique ecosystem where the river, lake, backwaters, and sea meet.
  • Activity: Take a serene motorboat ride through the dense, dark mangrove forests to reach the isolated Golden Sand Beach estuary.
09
The Capital City (30 km)
Poovar → Trivandrum
  • Morning: Drive to Thiruvananthapuram (Trivandrum), the historic capital of Kerala.
  • Temple: Visit the incredibly wealthy Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple (strict dress code: Mundus/Dhotis for men, Sarees for women).
  • Heritage: Explore the stunning Indo-Saracenic architecture of the Napier Museum and Kuthiramalika Palace.
  • Shopping: Buy authentic Kerala spices, cashew nuts, and handloom Kasavu sarees at the bustling Chalai Market.
10
Homeward Bound
Trivandrum → Surat
  • Morning: Enjoy a final classic Kerala breakfast of Puttu (steamed rice cake) and Kadala (black chickpea) curry.
  • Transit: Transfer to Trivandrum International Airport (TRV) or the Railway Station.
  • End of Trip: Return to Gujarat with unforgettable memories, rejuvenated by the slow pace of God's Own Country.

Flavours & Phrases

Food, Culture & Language

Kerala's cuisine is a love letter to coconut, spices, and the sea. Learn a few Malayalam phrases — locals will absolutely love the effort.

🐟

Karimeen Pollichathu

Pearl spot fish wrapped in banana leaf and slow-cooked with chilli, ginger, coconut paste. The queen of Kerala cuisine.

🍛

Kerala Sadhya

A 20+ dish vegetarian feast served on banana leaf. Rice with avial, sambar, payasam, thoran, pickles.

🥞

Appam & Stew

Lacy rice-flour hoppers with a crispy edge and soft centre, served with coconut milk vegetable or chicken stew.

🍌

Puttu & Kadala

Steamed rice cylinders layered with coconut, served with a robust black chickpea curry. The classic breakfast.

🍮

Palada Payasam

Creamy rice flakes kheer cooked in milk and sweetened condensed milk. A divine, comforting dessert.

Stay Smart

FAQs & Safety Guide

Kerala is highly literate and safe for tourists, but these tips will ensure a flawless 10-day trip without any hiccups.

Kerala temples are very strict regarding attire. Men must remove their shirts and wear a traditional Dhoti (Mundu). Women must wear long skirts, sarees, or Salwar Kameez. Pants, jeans, shorts, and dresses are strictly prohibited inside inner sanctums like the Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple.
Standard houseboats in Alleppey only turn on the Air Conditioning inside the bedrooms from 9:00 PM to 6:00 AM. During the day, you sit in the open-air shaded deck. If you want full-time AC while cruising, you must specifically book a "Premium/Luxury Full-Time AC" houseboat, which costs significantly more.
Alcohol is strictly regulated. You cannot buy it in normal supermarkets. It is only sold at government-run 'BEVCO' outlets (which have massive queues) or served in licensed 4/5-star hotels and beer parlors. Houseboats do not sell alcohol, but you are usually allowed to carry your own onboard.
The monsoon (June to August) transforms Kerala into a lush, vibrant green paradise, and is historically the best time for Ayurvedic treatments. However, heavy rains can cause landslides in the Munnar hills and disrupt houseboat operations. If you travel then, keep your itinerary flexible. The best standard season for sightseeing is October to March.
